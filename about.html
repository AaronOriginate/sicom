About SICOM

We build the networks that
carriers charge you to rent.

SICOM was founded on a simple observation: organisations across the UK were paying too much for connectivity they could own outright. We set out to change that equation — one fibre route at a time.

Our Story

Born from a frustration with
carrier pricing.

Steve Ives spent years watching organisations sign five-year leased line contracts, paying inflated monthly fees for bandwidth they could have owned for the cost of a single capital investment.

The maths never made sense to him. A managed circuit costs thousands per month, every month, forever. Dark fibre costs more upfront but pays for itself within three to five years — then delivers unlimited bandwidth for the next two decades at near-zero marginal cost.

So he built a team of specialists. Engineers who understood fibre optic design at a molecular level. Project managers who could route cables under motorways and through city centres without a single day of client downtime. A company that did one thing — dark fibre infrastructure — and did it better than anyone else in the region.

Our Approach

A proven methodology for
every project.

No surprises. No scope creep. Every SICOM project follows four clear stages so you know exactly what to expect from day one.

Understand Your Needs

We start by listening. What are you connecting? What bandwidth do you need now, and where will you be in ten years? We map your requirements before we map a single fibre route.

Design the Optimal Solution

Our engineers design a network architecture that balances performance, resilience and cost. You receive a detailed specification — not a vague proposal — before any work begins.

Install with Precision

Our in-house team handles every aspect of installation: civils, fibre blowing, splicing, testing and commissioning. Full project management with minimal disruption to your operations.

Support for the Long Term

Your infrastructure is a 25-year asset. We protect it with proactive monitoring, preventative maintenance and emergency response — so it performs at its best, year after year.
