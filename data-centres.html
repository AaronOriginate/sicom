The Challenge

Data centre operators face a unique set of connectivity pressures. Tenant demands are unpredictable, cross-connect costs erode margins, and every carrier dependency is a single point of failure you can't control.

Case Study — Multi-Site Data Centre Operator

Redundant dark fibre backbone replacing carrier-managed circuits across three facilities

A regional data centre operator was spending over six figures annually on carrier-managed circuits between three South West facilities. Bandwidth upgrades required lengthy contract renegotiations, and a single carrier outage had caused a four-hour service disruption affecting multiple tenants. SICOM designed and installed a redundant dark fibre ring connecting all three sites, giving the operator complete control over capacity allocation, eliminating monthly circuit costs, and providing a physical asset they own outright. The installation was completed in scheduled maintenance windows with zero impact on live tenant services.

Data Centre FAQs

Questions operators
always ask.

Every data centre installation follows our zero-downtime methodology. We work with your operations team to schedule all work within agreed maintenance windows. Cable routes are planned to avoid live infrastructure, and all fibre blowing, splicing and testing happens without any impact on existing services. Our team carries full DBS checks and complies with your facility's security and access protocols — we've worked in enough data centres to know that trust is non-negotiable.
We design for the highest density environments in the market. Our structured cabling solutions support 288-fibre trunk cables, high-density patch panels with up to 144 ports per rack unit, and pre-terminated MTP/MPO solutions for rapid deployment. Whether you're running 10G, 40G or 100G per port, we design the physical layer to match your current requirements and provide a clear upgrade path as demand grows. We'll also model your anticipated growth so the infrastructure scales without re-cabling.
All our multi-site backbone designs use physically diverse routes — meaning the primary and secondary fibre paths follow completely separate cable routes with no shared ducting, manholes or points of failure. For critical infrastructure we specify a ring topology, so traffic can route in either direction if a single fibre path is cut. We also incorporate OTDR monitoring at key splice points so we can detect degradation or physical interference before it causes a service-affecting fault.
